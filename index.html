Outside of high school, I volunteered around the community which led to me becoming engaged in the disparities between people. I wanted to make a change within the stigmas and cultural stereotypes between all races and ethnicities. Because this is something I was passionate about, towards the end of my junior year, I signed up for two internships that intersected with my interests. I joined Latino Service Providers: Testimonios Project as a Youth Promotor, also known as a community health worker. This internship exposed me to vulnerable topics the Latinx Community is shamed for talking about. Along with this internship, I also joined the Sonoma County Junior Commission on Human Rights. Within this internship, I have assisted in creating and organizing an event surrounding immigrant rights. This project has allowed me to educate at the root level and expand knowledge of factual information within the community of Sonoma County. Both of these internships have ingrained a self-awareness of empowerment and pushed me to seek higher education.

Currently, I am a part of a tutoring program here at UCR to try and motivate young students to continue their education. Being a part of this program has allowed me to advocate for educational support for young students of color who are disproportionately behind due to the lack of services in their schools and communities.
